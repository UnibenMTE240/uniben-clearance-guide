Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common clearance problems faced by UNIBEN 100-level students.

  • What if my JAMB name is incorrect?

    Apply for correction of data via the JAMB portal. Visit any JAMB office or accredited CBT center. UNIBEN may reject clearance if names don’t match.

  • What if my WAEC name is incorrect?

    If the name on your WAEC result is incorrect, your clearance may be declined. You’ll need to visit a WAEC office to request a correction before you can be successfully cleared.

  • Do I need to upload WAEC/NECO scratch card?

    Yes. UNIBEN uses it to verify your result. Upload the pin and serial number clearly with your WAEC/NECO certificate.

  • Can I re-upload a rejected document?

    Yes. If your portal status goes back to "Clearance Started", you can re-upload the corrected version.

  • My clearance is stuck on “Requested”?

    This means it’s under review. Be patient — it may take over a month.

  • I haven't received my Kofa login?

    First accept your admission on JAMB CAPS. Then check your Aspirant Profile for login details.

  • What if my birth certificate is a declaration of age?

    UNIBEN only accepts a proper birth certificate from the National Population Commission (NPC). Declaration of Age is not accepted for clearance.

  • Is attestation letter the same as guarantor's letter?

    No. UNIBEN requires a Guarantor’s Letter. An attestation letter or general character reference will be rejected during clearance.

  • Who writes the Acceptance Letter?

    The acceptance letter is written by you (the student). It's a formal letter stating that you accept the admission offered to you by UNIBEN.

  • What happens if I upload a blurry or wrong document?

    Your clearance may be rejected. Make sure your scans are colored, clear, and properly named before uploading to avoid delays.

  • Is medical clearance compulsory?

    Yes. All newly admitted students must undergo a medical screening at the UNIBEN health center after completing their online clearance.

  • When and where do I get the Record File?

    After online clearance, pay ₦3,000 via Remita, take the receipt to Bursary for conversion, then collect the record file from Exams & Records. You’ll be given 3 copies. Learn more on how to get your record file.

  • Do I need to complete online clearance before registering courses?

    Physical Course Registration can be done even before your online clearance gets approved. While Online Course Registration is only available after your online clearance has been approved on the Kofa portal. Learn more about course registration.

  • My passport background is blue instead of red, is it acceptable?

    No. UNIBEN requires recent passport photographs with a red background. Using the wrong background can lead to clearance rejection.

  • My WAEC/NECO certificate is not ready. Can I use a result slip?

    UNIBEN requires the original WAEC certificate, not the result slip. If yours isn’t ready, you may need to wait until it is issued. UNIBEN do not require the original NECO certificate if it is not available.

  • How long does online clearance take?

    It varies. If your documents are correct and clear, it may take a few days. Errors or heavy submission traffic can delay it by a week or more.

  • What should I do if I mistakenly uploaded the wrong document?

    Wait for your status to change back to “Clearance Started” so you can re-upload. Avoid repeating the same mistake to prevent further delays.

  • I can’t see the “Upload Documents” section on Kofa. What should I do?

    Ensure your acceptance fee has been paid and successfully processed. If the section is still hidden after payment, check back in a few hours.

  • Is there a deadline for clearance?

    No. there is no deadline for physical clearance. But try to do yours on time.

  • Can I change or update a document after being cleared?

    No. Once you’ve been cleared, document changes are no longer allowed. Double-check all uploads before submitting them the first time.

  • I accepted admission on JAMB CAPS, but my UNIBEN Kofa login isn’t showing?

    Sometimes it can take longer than expected after accepting admission for your Kofa login to be created. Check your aspirant profile regularly. If it takes longer, please try to be patient.
