Document Upload Guidelines

To complete your UNIBEN online clearance, you must upload all required documents correctly and clearly.

Why it matters: Blurry or incorrect documents may lead to clearance delays or outright rejection. Take time to prepare them properly.

All documents must be:

  • Clearly scanned or downloaded in PDF/JPG format
  • In color (black & white scans may be rejected)
  • Placed in the correct upload slots on your portal

Start with First Document

Required Documents for Online Clearance

Here is a complete list of documents you must prepare and upload for your UNIBEN 100L online clearance. Ensure all are properly scanned and meet the specified requirements.

  • JAMB Admission Letter (Institution Copy)
  • JAMB Result Slip
  • Acceptance Letter (written by student)
  • Guarantor’s Letter
  • Birth Certificate (Declaration of Age not accepted)
  • Local Government of Origin Certificate
  • WAEC/NECO Certificate (not result slip)
  • WAEC/NECO Scratch Card (with valid PIN or token)
  • Passport Photograph (red background)
  • Affidavit of Non-Membership of Secret Cult
  • Affidavit of Good Conduct

Upload only clear, colored scans of each document. Blurry, cropped, or mismatched files can delay your clearance.

JAMB Admission Letter Institution Copy

The Institution Copy of your JAMB Admission Letter is required for online clearance. It contains official details about your admission and must be downloaded from the JAMB eFacility portal.

How to Get It:
  • Visit JAMB eFacility Portal
  • Log in with your JAMB email and password
  • Click “Print Admission Letter”
  • Pay ₦1,000 via Remita to download the letter in PDF
Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Submitting the Candidate Copy instead of Institution Copy
  • Uploading screenshots or blurry scans
  • Black-and-white versions — use color PDF or colored scan

Sample JAMB Admission Letter (Institution Copy)

JAMB Result Slip

The JAMB Result Slip shows your UTME score and personal details. This document is required for your clearance and must be the official version downloaded from JAMB’s portal.

How to Get It:
  • Visit JAMB eFacility Portal
  • Log in with your JAMB email and password
  • Click “Print Result Slip” on your dashboard
  • Pay ₦1,500 via Remita or any other payment method given to download the official PDF copy
Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Uploading a screenshot or camera photo of your result
  • Submitting black and white or cropped scans
  • Using an outdated or incomplete result slip
Sample JAMB Result Slip

Student Acceptance Letter

This is a short formal letter written by the student to the University of Benin, stating that you have accepted the admission offered to you. It should be typed, signed, and scanned clearly.

What to Include:
  • Date (top right corner)
  • Student’s Full Name and JAMB Registration Number
  • Course of Study and Faculty
  • A statement of acceptance (e.g., “I hereby accept the provisional admission offered to me by the University of Benin.”)
  • Student’s Signature at the bottom
Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Uploading handwritten letters (must be typed)
  • Forgetting to sign the letter before scanning
  • Missing name, JAMB reg. number, or date

Sample Acceptance Letter (typed, signed, and scanned)

Guarantor’s Letter

UNIBEN requires a Guarantor’s Letter written and signed by a reputable person who can vouch for your character and conduct. This is different from a general attestation letter and must meet official standards.

What is Accepted:
  • Typed letter addressed to The Registrar, University of Benin
  • Includes guarantor's full name, address, occupation, and phone number
  • Stamped and signed with guarantor’s ID attached (if possible)
  • Scanned clearly in color
Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Uploading a handwritten or informal letter
  • Submitting an attestation letter (UNIBEN does not accept it)
  • Missing signature or no contact details of the guarantor

Sample Guarantor’s Letter (Typed and Signed)

Birth Certificate

A valid Birth Certificate is required to confirm your date of birth during clearance. It must be issued by a recognized authority such as the National Population Commission or your local government registry.

What is Accepted:
  • Original Birth Certificate from the National Population Commission or LGA
  • Clearly scanned in color with official seal and signature visible
Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Submitting a court affidavit (Declaration of Age) — not accepted
  • Uploading faded or blurry scans
  • Missing stamp or incomplete certificate

Local Government of Origin Certificate

This certificate confirms the local government area and state you’re officially from. It must be issued by your local government council and contain their official stamp and signature.

What is Accepted:
  • Original Certificate issued by your local government headquarters
  • Must include your full name, LGA, and official stamp
  • Scanned in full color (not photocopied)
Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Uploading without the LGA stamp or seal
  • Using a general certificate of indigene (without official header)
  • Submitting a black-and-white photocopy or blurry image

Sample Local Government of Origin Certificate

WAEC / NECO Certificate

UNIBEN requires your original WAEC or NECO certificate as proof of academic qualification. Result slips are not accepted for clearance.

What is Accepted:
  • WAEC Certificate (issued by WAEC)
  • NECO Certificate (issued by NECO)
  • Clearly scanned in color (full document, not cropped)
Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Submitting a result slip instead of the certificate
  • Uploading a blurry or partial scan
  • Submitting a photocopy or black-and-white scan

Sample WAEC Certificate

WAEC / NECO Scratch Card

UNIBEN requires your WAEC or NECO scratch card to verify the authenticity of your result during the clearance process. The card must match the exam body whose certificate you uploaded.

What is Accepted:
  • WAEC PIN Card (e.g., WAEC Result Checker card)
  • NECO Token (purchased from NECO or approved vendors)
  • PIN/Token must be visible and valid (not previously exhausted)
  • Scanned or clearly photographed (with readable details)
Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Uploading a used/exhausted card
  • Submitting a PIN/token that doesn't match your uploaded result
  • Hiding or blurring the PIN on the card

Passport Photograph

You are required to upload a recent passport photograph with a red background. This will be used for identification throughout your time in UNIBEN, so make sure it’s clear and professional.

What is Accepted:
  • Photograph with red background only
  • Recent photo (taken within the last 6 months)
  • Full face visible, no hats, filters, or sunglasses
  • Format: JPG or PNG
Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Using blue, white, or mixed background
  • Uploading a selfie or camera photo instead of a proper passport
  • Cropped or stretched images

Affidavit of Non-Membership of Secret Cult

As part of the clearance process, UNIBEN requires students to swear an affidavit confirming they are not a member of any secret cult, confraternity, or unlawful group. This is a strict university policy to promote peace and discipline on campus.

How to Get It:
  • Visit any High Court or Magistrate Court in your area
  • Request an Affidavit of Non-Membership of Secret Cult
  • Ensure your full name and declaration are clearly stated
  • It must be stamped and signed by the commissioner for oaths
Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Uploading affidavits without an official court stamp
  • Submitting general-purpose affidavits
  • Cropped or unclear scans

Sample Affidavit of Non-Membership of Secret Cult

Affidavit of Good Conduct

This affidavit declares that you will abide by the rules and regulations of the University of Benin. It is a personal commitment to good behavior, required as part of your online clearance documentation.

How to Get It:
  • Visit any High Court or Magistrate Court
  • Request an Affidavit of Good Conduct
  • It should contain your full name and a declaration of good behavior
  • Ensure it is stamped and signed by a commissioner for oaths
Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Submitting a document without court stamp or signature
  • Uploading a blurry or cropped scan
  • Submitting a general-purpose affidavit without specific content

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

  • When will my Kofa portal account be ready?
    Your Kofa account is automatically created after you accept your admission on JAMB CAPS. It may take a few hours or days — check your aspirant profile regularly.
  • Where can I find my Kofa username and password?
    You can find them on your aspirant profile page on the UNIBEN portal after accepting your JAMB admission.
  • Can I use a Declaration of Age instead of a Birth Certificate?
    No. UNIBEN only accepts original birth certificates. Declaration of Age is not accepted.
  • Is a result slip accepted instead of WAEC/NECO certificate?
    No. You must upload the original WAEC/NECO certificate. Result slips are not accepted.
  • Do I need to upload my WAEC/NECO scratch card?
    Yes. UNIBEN uses it to verify your result. Ensure your PIN/token is visible and valid.
  • What type of passport photograph should I upload?
    Upload a recent passport photo with a red background. Do not upload selfies, casual photos, or black-and-white images.
  • Is a Guarantor’s Letter the same as an Attestation Letter?
    No. UNIBEN accepts only Guarantor’s Letters. Attestation letters are not allowed.
  • Can I use screenshots or camera photos of documents?
    No. Upload only clear scans or original PDF versions of your documents — not screenshots or camera photos.
  • What happens if I upload a wrong or unclear document?
    Your clearance could be delayed or rejected. Always scan in color, and make sure the document is clear and complete.
  • I can’t see the acceptance fee payment link. What should I do?
    The payment portal is activated after your Kofa account is created. If it hasn't shown yet, give it some time and check back later.
  • What if my name is incorrect on JAMB CAPS?
    You need to visit a JAMB office or accredited center for correction. Clearance may be delayed if your name on JAMB doesn’t match your other documents.
  • What if my name is incorrect on my WAEC certificate?
    You’ll need to apply for a name correction through WAEC. Clearance may be delayed if there’s a name mismatch across your documents.
  • Where can I ask for help?
    You can join the official clearance support WhatsApp group or revisit the help guides on this site. If you’re still stuck, contact UNIBEN ICT.
